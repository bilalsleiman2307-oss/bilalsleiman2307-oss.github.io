Frequently asked questions about locksmith service in Berlin

How quickly can the locksmith arrive?

Our usual arrival time throughout Berlin is 10–30 minutes. We give you a realistic estimate when you contact us.

How much does a door opening cost?

Door opening starts from €59. The final price depends on the door situation and the work required, and is discussed before complex work begins.

Does the price from €59 apply to a door opening?

Yes. €59 is the starting price for a door opening, not an unrestricted fixed price for every door or situation.

Can a slammed door be opened without damage?

Yes. If the door has only slammed shut and has not been locked, we open it without damage.

What happens if I have lost my keys?

We can open the door and assess the security risk. A lock or cylinder is changed only when necessary and after you agree to the work and price.

What happens if the key has broken in the lock?

We inspect the broken key and cylinder, then explain whether removal, door opening or another agreed repair is required.

Is the locksmith available at night, on weekends and public holidays?

Yes. Trust Schlüsseldienst Berlin is available 24 hours a day, seven days a week.

Do I need to show an ID?

Yes. Access authorization is checked with an ID before or after opening. Alternatively, a neighbour can confirm that you live at the address.

Is a new profile cylinder included in the door-opening price?

No. A new profile cylinder is not automatically included. Any necessary material and its price are agreed before installation.

Does a key left inside make the door opening more expensive?

No. A key left or inserted on the inside does not change our door-opening price.

Which Berlin districts do you cover?

We serve all of Berlin, including Gesundbrunnen, Wedding, Mitte, Pankow, Prenzlauer Berg and Reinickendorf.

Can I contact you via WhatsApp?

Yes. You can call or send us a WhatsApp message at any time.

Need an English-speaking locksmith in Berlin?

Call now or send a WhatsApp message. Tell us your location and whether the door is slammed shut, locked or affected by a key problem.
